I often confronted with the thought **How do I teach my kid to do the right thing?**, now that a loaded question could be broken down into many different sub-question. But I often bothered me!

What combination of words should I use to make him do that right thing? I know if he eats too many chocolates it bad for his health, he will get addicted to the chocolate and he is going to demand it again, which I will had hard-time refusing it. So, the solution is to *just refuse?*, but well if I refuse everything then am I not depriving him from the experiences? Who doesn't like chocolates?

But a different question springs out. If its me who is stopping him to eat chocolates what if I am not around? That means he should be able to reach that decision by himself! Self moderation is the right answer perhaps. But is really that easy for him to reach the wisdom of self-moderation?

Avoid chocolates how?

I came across an interesting quote from Einstein, *Humans are not wise enough to learn from other peoples experience*.
